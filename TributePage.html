Aretha Franklin

Queen of Soul

Aretha Franklin singing while playing piano

An abbreviated list of accomplishments

  • First woman to be inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1987
  • Ranked number 1 on the Rolling Stone  list of Greatest Singers of All Time
  • Received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 1979
  • Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom on November 9, 2005
  • Recipient of the National Medal of Arts in 1999
  • Inducted to the GMA Gospel Music Hall of Fame in 2012
  • Became the most charted female in history with 112 charted singles on  Billboard, 17 top-ten pop singles, 100 R&B entries, and 20 number-one R&B singles

A few notable performances

  • Singing at Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.’s funeral in 1968
  • Preforming for Pope Francis at the 2015 World meeting of Families
  • Singing at President Obama’s 2009 inauguration
  • Filling in last minute for Pavarotti at the 1998 Grammys
